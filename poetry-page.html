Ode to the Masked Boy

A boy with a mask,

Stuck to his face like it was melted on.

People say he lived too fast.

Moments of his youth felt like centuries,

The outside saw the way time truly flew,

As his alarm clock was thrown out the window.

It took no time for

The world to crack the boy’s mask,

It was close to shattering into nothingness.

So he took off the mask

And the boy was no more.

The boy buried in a man’s memories

As he washes the blood off the mask.

Looking into the mirror,

To catch the last tear

Shed by the boy before he is set free.

The moment passes as the outside forgets him.

Jane's Eulogy

“I woke up and found her, that's all I know”, is what I was told to say

But that wasn’t all I knew, and it ate me up inside.

Even now, I can still feel the numbing of that last needle

I listen to her voicemail for the last time before her number gets

Disconnected.

The cold gaze she had while she was lying there like some sort of doll

Left an imprint in my brain like it was one of her paintings.

Her eyes were telling the novel of her death,

I know they were trying to tell me something but I was too blind with grief to read.

When I woke up,

I felt the reaper’s touch on my back quickly leave.

Dazed and

Confused, the

High was

Overwhelming as it

Crashed

Down.

But when I noticed her, the rush of adrenaline I felt got me higher than anything before

I lept on top of her with both hands pressed against her chest

I push

And push

As I pray for her to wake up.

Praying for this nightmare to be exactly what I wish it to be, a nightmare.

I break down into her chest

I wasn’t saving her life, just trying to catch wind in my hands.

As I cry, in hysterics, looking at her lifeless expression

I allow myself to become detached,

Plunging deeper into the coldest crevice to find my soul,

As I leave the depths of reality.

When I look at her, I see myself in the reflection of her eyes.

“I killed her. It was me.” I sobbed to the man who was like a father to me.

To a man who held me like I was his son.

To a man who I barely knew

And as he held me, I felt it again.

The reaper’s touch.

As The Days Go By

There exists

Blank pieces inside this blanket of memories.

Afternoon dinner with estranged family

Blank faces expressing emotions,

And lonely rides together.

As days go by

My youth’s plot echoes

Themes of doubt that burn my soul.

“How long till the end?”

I pondered, walking a path of uncertainty.

Walking until

An ocean of doubt flooded

Into

The gates of my hell.

As days go by,

I drifted down the water

As still as a widow's tear.

But then, I met her,

This angel fish,

She flew through the water

Enchanting me

To follow her.

This water started to feel like air, so I began

To swim to her.

Every breath I missed only pushed my

Arms closer to her.

I imagined she would be up there

Floating, waiting for me.

But when I made it to the surface,

She was nowhere to be found.

As days go by,

I float like driftwood wandering

While wondering

The only thing I could see in any direction though was

Water.

No sight of her.

I look up to see

Her in the sky watching me float,

Enchanting me

To come follow her.

But I had no wings

Not like her.

Now

To pass time

I watch her, in awe,

Fly so majestically.

As the days go by.

Dear Fear

The way you take control

Has left me a remote version of who I use to be.

Just ease up a bit and let me channel my confidence

Before I change altogether.

Now I get more regrets than I do mail,

And as a male, it's expected that I should overcome you easily

but it gets harder to understand what you want

I want to say whatever I want,

I want to express how I feel,

I want to be who I am but I can't as long as you're here.

Because whenever you get in my head

I become frantic

And your voice tiptoes to my thoughts

Whispering "Hey man, get calm you need some

Communism,”

And then you take control.

Response

I feel it, it's here,

I hear it, it's coming closer,

I have to go now, I have to start running

towards it,

Towards it all

I think i feel it.

It's beautiful, this feeling,

Like seeing a sunrise on a cold morning on a summer beach,

I feel like I know it all,

That I know everything will be okay.

I like this feeling,

This feeling of comfort.

I feel the need to run towards it,

Because it feels like it's something meaningful.

But I think this feeling is fleeting.

It has to be

Just like all good things,

But it's alright,

Cause this feeling is just one play button away.
